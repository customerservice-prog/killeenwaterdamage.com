Licensed Mold Remediation in Killeen, TX

Mold is the symptom, not the first problem—there was almost always moisture first. In Killeen, that is often a slow window seal leak, a flex line behind a rental washer, or a roof nail pop after a May hail run. We build containment, run true HEPA air scrubbers (not a hardware-store air cleaner with a HEPA label), and remove materials that will not return to a dry, stable condition. This page is about what a proper remediation job looks like on the ground, not a scary photo gallery, because you already have enough to worry about if you are searching this at 2 a.m. after a baby woke up coughing.

Why “spray and pray” fails in high humidity

A fogger or a coat of Kills over sheetrock in a 65 percent relative-humidity week does not change the fact that the paper face of drywall is a food source and the cavity is still wet. The Texas Department of State Health Services (and plain sense) point toward source removal in most cellulose materials when contamination is not surface-only. We are not a franchise running the same 12-step jingle; we are a local crew that knows Killeen insulation codes, which neighborhoods still have old poly pipes, and which apartment managers want written clearance letters before reoccupancy.

Landlords in Temple or Cove sometimes try quick wipe-downs after Category 1 leaks in tenant units. If a tenant has asthma or a newborn, the conversation shifts from cosmetic to indoor air quality fast. We give you a plain-English work plan, not a Latin species list, unless a hygienist is involved.

Spotted fuzzy growth on drywall after the leak you thought you fixed? Call before you rip open walls yourself and spread spores into the return air path.

When to call us immediately

  • There was standing water in the home or crawlspace in the last week and the wall still looks stained or feels soft.
  • You or a family member is suddenly experiencing respiratory irritation in one room, especially after the AC cycles on with moldy insulation smell.
  • Renters: your landlord is scheduling handyman patch work without a moisture map—document first, then get an independent look.
  • Storm-driven roof leaks in spring hail season wet attic sheathing you cannot see from the living room; dark spotting at ceiling corners is a flag.
  • Any mold behind a main electrical panel (rare, but it happens in exterior walls)—do not demo live circuits yourself.

Our process (4 steps)

  1. Assess and scope under containment design. We determine whether materials are non-porous, semi-, or fully porous, and set negative air to protect clean rooms, kids, and pets. Clear photos go to the carrier when coverage applies—especially important with USAA and Farmers in Texas, who often ask for before-and-in-progress shots.
  2. Remove the wet and the non-salvageable. Bag-out under negative pressure, HEPA-vac, damp-wipe per protocol, not random tear-outs that bill by the hour with no end date.
  3. Dry the assembly. Sometimes we partner with our drying team the same day; sometimes we return after active leaks are 100% fixed so we are not remediating into new moisture.
  4. Post-remediation check. Visual clearance or third-party spore test per your request and policy language; we do not “write our own A+ grade” on lab choice unless you ask for that convenience.

Texas is not the same as Gulf Coast stucco buildings; our ranch slabs and stud bays dry differently, so your cousin’s experience in Houston is not 1:1. We measure and note where vapor barriers were skipped in older Killeen remodels, because that changes where mold returns if a neighbor fixes only face layers.

What insurance typically covers (Texas)

Carriers use different sublimits, endorsements, and time limits for fungus and microbial coverage. Allstate’s endorsements, State Farm’s water backup riders, and Farmers’ multi-peril language are not identical—read your form number. We tie mold work to a documented water event where possible, because a tied claim is more defensible than “I think it has been that way for years.”

We do not guarantee coverage, but we do know what documentation typically survives desk review, so you are not stuck paying out of pocket when you did not have to.

Local questions we get every week

  • Third-party test first?

    Sometimes, especially for disputes. Many jobs are scoped off visible and moisture data after a water loss we already mitigated.

  • Is all black mold toxic?

    We focus on source control and safe removal, not just color. Lab ID helps when you need it.

  • Will my policy pay?

    Depends on sudden loss vs. long-term; sublimits and endorsements apply—your adjuster decides.

  • How long is move-out required?

    Often you can stay; we engineer containment to seal the work zone. Full-house losses differ.

  • AC concerns?

    We coordinate duct and equipment isolation so the rest of the system is not cross-contaminated when avoidable.
