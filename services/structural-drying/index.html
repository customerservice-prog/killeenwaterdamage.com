Structural Drying & Dehumidification in Killeen, TX

The water is gone from the top of the tile, but your baseboards are still reading wet. That is the stage where a lot of do-it-yourself efforts stall—and where mold and swelling trim show up. Structural drying is the science of moving moisture out of wall cavities, chases, and subfloors using controlled airflow, temperature, and dehumidification, then proving it is dry to an insurance-grade standard. Why Killeen homes do not “air dry” on their own

We sit in a hot-humid climate for months at a time. When your AC is fighting 95-degree afternoons, the interior air can still be above the dew point in a wet wall cavity. Truckloads of big-box fans in the living room only stir room air; they do not always drop the vapor pressure inside a north-facing bedroom exterior wall. Our crews measure grains per pound and equilibrium moisture content in wood, not guess from touch. If you are in a duplex off Fort Hood traffic corridors, you also deal with more overnight noise restrictions—we schedule equipment to balance drying speed with neighbor complaints.

Slab homes common here hide moisture along plumbing runs and under LVP installed straight on concrete. Two-story townhomes in Harker Heights can show ceiling stains from upstairs toilet wax failures days after the floor felt “dry.” We use non-destructive scanning first, then small inspection ports when the assembly is closed and drying needs directed airflow.

Meters still beeping in the hall bath? Call before you seal new paint or lay flooring over damp OSB. Trapped moisture is what turns a $3,000 dry-out into a $20,000 mold tear-out later.

When to call us immediately

  • Carpet squelches, pad is saturated, or you see new cupping in hardwood and laminate after an extraction-only visit.
  • Sheetrock is wicking a foot or more from the floor line, or bubbling tape at seams in closets that share wet-wall plumbing.
  • You smell a sweet or earthy odor two days after you thought the leak was fixed—often hidden chase moisture.
  • Insurance told you to “get drying estimates” after an approved mitigation scope—we align line items to their desk review.
  • Any delay beyond 24 hours in summer humidity: mold clock starts, not a scare tactic—an IICRC fact.

Our process (4 steps)

  1. Inspect and set dry standard. We agree on target moisture content and document pre-loss when possible, especially in disputed appliance-line claims with carriers like Allstate and Farmers in Texas.
  2. Install drying system. Low-grain dehus, air movers, specialty mats, or injectidry for cabinets—zoned to hit wet layers without over-drying art or books left in the room if we can work around them.
  3. Monitor daily. We adjust equipment and upload readings. If a room is not progressing, we find hidden moisture paths—HVAC plenums, base shoe channels, or missing vapor barrier on slab edges.
  4. Release and handoff. We issue a drying log you can hand to a rebuild contractor or your adjuster, with photos of any materials that must be removed because EMC stayed too high for salvaging.

Drying is iterative. A cheap quote that “guarantees three days” without data is a red flag. We quote ranges and show you the psychrometry chart so you understand why a cooler snap or a gulf front through central Texas can change the schedule overnight.

What insurance typically covers (Texas)

Drying and dehumidification that directly follow a covered sudden water event are often included under dwelling coverage, subject to your deductible. USAA and State Farm both issue moisture mitigation scopes we match with industry-standard dehu-hour calculations when adjusters use Xactimate price lists. If your policy has an endorsement limiting “further damage” language, we flag it before you commit to 10 more air movers.

Long-term maintenance moisture—slow plumbing pinholes, unvented bath fans—is harder to get covered; drying may still be medically necessary, but the dollar source differs. We never promise a carrier will pay, but we give them defensible data.

Questions we hear a lot in Bell County

  • How long does structural drying take?

    Usually 3 to 5 days; complex assemblies or crawlspaces can be longer, with clear daily numbers.

  • Can I sleep with equipment on?

    We can sometimes rotate quieter cycles overnight; full shutdown usually adds days.

  • Will my hardwood be saved?

    Many engineered and solid products can be dried in place if cupping is fresh; if not, we help document replacement for your claim.

  • What proof do insurers want?

    Photos, equipment logs, and moisture grids—our standard deliverable to Texas carriers.
